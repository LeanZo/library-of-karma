### **Description:**

*Dark Water* is a haunting Japanese horror film directed by Hideo Nakata and released in 2002. Known for its eerie atmosphere and psychological tension, the film is a standout example of J-Horror. It's a gripping and atmospheric tale that explores themes of motherhood and supernatural mysteries.

***

### **Summary:**

In *Dark Water*, a recently divorced mother, Yoshimi, moves into a dilapidated apartment building with her young daughter, Ikuko. As they settle into their new life, they notice strange occurrences, including water leaks from the ceiling and a mysterious red bag left behind by a previous tenant. These odd events escalate, leading Yoshimi to uncover a dark and unsettling secret associated with the building.
The film delves into the psychological and emotional turmoil experienced by Yoshimi as she grapples with the paranormal while trying to protect her daughter. With its slow-building tension and evocative visuals, *Dark Water* creates a chilling and atmospheric narrative that will keep you on the edge of your seat. It's a psychological horror masterpiece that skillfully explores the blurred lines between the supernatural and the psychological.
