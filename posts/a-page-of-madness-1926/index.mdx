### **Description:**

*A Page of Madness* is a groundbreaking silent Japanese film directed by Teinosuke Kinugasa, released in 1926 during the era of silent cinema. This avant-garde masterpiece is celebrated for its innovative storytelling and stunning visual imagery. It stands as an early example of Japanese cinema's artistic evolution and showcases the power of non-verbal storytelling.

***

### **Summary:**

Set in a mental asylum, *A Page of Madness* follows the life of a janitor who takes a job at the institution to be close to his institutionalized wife. As he navigates the harrowing and surreal world inside the asylum, the film delves into the complexities of the human mind and the blurred lines between reality and illusion. With its mesmerizing visual effects and expressionist style, the film explores themes of love, loss, and the thin boundaries between sanity and madness. *A Page of Madness* is a captivating cinematic experience that challenges traditional narrative conventions and offers a haunting glimpse into the human psyche.
